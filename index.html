  1. History
    1. Background

      2. Cricket was created during Saxon or Norman times by children living in the Weald,
      an area of dense woodlands and clearings in south-east England that lies across Kent and Sussex.

    2. Foundation

      3. A cricket foundation can refer to various organizations involved in promoting and supporting
      the sport of cricket. This can include foundations focused on developing young talent, providing
      coaching and training, or supporting the overall growth of cricket within a community or region.

    3. Expansions and termination

      4. Expansion generally refers to the growth of the game, both geographically and in terms
      of the number of teams or formats.Termination refers to how an innings or a match comes to
      an end, most commonly through dismissals of batters or reaching a set number of overs.

    3. Match rules

      4. The batting team aims to score runs by hitting the ball and running between the wickets,
      while the fielding team tries to dismiss the batsmen and restrict their scoring. The team
      with the most runs at the end of the match wins.

    4. Prize money

      5. The average prize money in cricket varies significantly based on the tournament and
      the player's involvement (international vs. domestic, central contract status, etc.).

  4. Teams

    1. Current teams

      2. BCCI Domestic Teams are the 38 regional and institutional cricket teams that
      compete in India's official domestic cricket competitions, governed by the Board of
      Control for Cricket in India (BCCI).

    2. Defaunct team

      3. Cricket teams, both at the domestic and international level, have become defunct over time.
      These teams include those that participated in the Ranji Trophy, the IPL, and other competitions.
      Some ceased to exist due to mergers of Indian states, while others were disbanded due to financial issues.

    3. Timeline

      4. The time period of a cricket game varies greatly depending on the format,
      ranging from a few hours to five days. Limited overs matches,
      like T20s, are completed in a few hours, while One Day Internationals (ODIs)
      can take around 7-8 hours.
